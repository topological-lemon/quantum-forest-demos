Aggregating a multilayer ecological network to a single layer retains only approximately 52% of community structure information.

Pilosof, S., Porter, M. A., Pascual, M., & Kéfi, S. (2017). The multilayer nature of ecological networks. Nature Ecology & Evolution, 1, 0101. doi:10.1038/s41559-017-0101

This four-species network is schematic — it illustrates the structural argument, not a specific empirical dataset. In real tropical ecosystems the interaction network spans thousands of species across multiple simultaneous layers, and the information loss from aggregation is correspondingly more severe.
