The orangutan's three-level recursive call

Sumatran female orangutans encode predator-specific information across three hierarchical levels of the same alarm call. Toggle between a natural predator (tiger) and a non-natural predator (abstract pattern) to hear and see the difference.

De Gregorio, C., Gamba, M., & Lameira, A. R. (2025). Third-order self-embedded vocal motifs in wild orangutans, and the selective evolution of recursion. Annals of the New York Academy of Sciences, 1549, 219–229. doi:10.1111/nyas.15373

Isochrony at the call-combination level was statistically significant for natural predator responses (p = 0.001) but not for non-natural predator responses (p = 0.291). Bout tempo differed significantly between conditions (p = 0.031). Series-level timing showed no significant difference between conditions.
